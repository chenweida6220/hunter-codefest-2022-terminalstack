CardioPulmonary Resuscitation

Below, you can find a video on how to perform CPR


To perform hands only CPR:

  1. Ensure that the scene is safe.
  2. If the person is unresponsive, check for breathing and life threatening conditions such as bleeding. Shout their name and tap their shoulders to check for response.
  3. If the person doesn't respond, call 911.
  4. Lay the person on their back on a flat surface.
  5. Give chest compressions with the following conditions:
    • Hands centered on the chest
    • Shoulders directly over your hands, with elbows locked
    • Push down on their chest at least 2 inches deep
    • Rate is within the range of 100 to 120 beats per minute
      • Note: the tempo of Bee Gee's "Stayin' Alive" is approximately 103 beats per minute
    • Allow the chest to return to normal position after each compression

To perform mouth to mouth as well:

  1. Give 2 breaths.
    • Open the airway by gently tilting the person's head back with one hand and lifting their chin with the other.
    • Make sure that each breath lasts around 1 second and causes the chest to rise.
    • Before giving the next breath, allow the air to exit
  2. Repeat this pattern -- give 30 chest compressions and then 2 breaths of air.
